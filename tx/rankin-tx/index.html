Signs of Pest Infestation

Pest infestations can cause significant damage to your property and pose health risks. At Pemdem Pest Control, we help residents across Rankin Texas detect the early signs of pest infestations before they spiral out of control.
One major indicator is the presence of droppings or shed skin. Whether it’s rodents, cockroaches, or other pests, these remnants are telltale signs that pests have invaded. Another sign to watch for is grease marks or tracks along walls, as rodents tend to travel the same routes, leaving greasy stains behind.
Strange noises at night like scratching or scurrying sounds in walls or ceilings often signal a rodent infestation. Similarly, damaged food packaging in your pantry may indicate pests such as insects or rodents have gained access to your food supplies.
Don’t overlook property damage, such as chewed wires or holes in wooden structures. Termites, in particular, can silently wreak havoc on your home’s foundation. Rankin, TX Insights: Population, Influence, and Service Areas

Rankin is a city in Upton County, Texas, United States. Its population was 778 at the 2010 census. It is named after F.E. Rankin, a local rancher. It is the county seat of the thinly populated county; in 2010, only 3,355 people lived in the entire county, and McCamey was the only larger town. During the early 20th century, the town grew due to the discovery of oil in the nearby Yates Oil Field. However, since a second oil boom in the '80s, the town has seen a decline in its economy and population.
